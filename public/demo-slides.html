The Problem

Finance information is expensive, scattered,
and locked inside closed networks.

💸
Paywalled intelligence
A Bloomberg terminal costs $25,000/year. Institutional research is inaccessible. Students and junior professionals fly blind while senior folks have every data point.
→ "I had no idea Ares was hiring until the role was filled."
🔍
Fragmented information
Hiring signals, market trends, interview prep, and industry news are scattered across LinkedIn, Reddit, newsletters, firm sites, and Slack groups with no single home.
→ Professionals spend hours weekly just aggregating info.
🚪
Closed networks
The best intelligence — which firms are actively deploying, who's hiring, what's actually happening in deals — travels through insider alumni networks most people can't access.
→ First-generation finance professionals are most disadvantaged.
